## Key Reasoning Notes

**The homepage problem.** The homepage currently ranks for 12 clusters including high-volume commercial queries like "Marketing Automation Software For Startups" (7,335 vol, pos 29.5) and "Marketing Automation Tools For Agencies" (2,480 vol, pos 16.6). These are not incidental rankings — they're mismatch rankings. The homepage cannot convert users who landed on it searching for a startup-specific guide or an agency tools roundup. Creating dedicated pages for these queries will likely both improve rankings and conversion rates.

**The workflow post is overloaded.** `/blog/marketing-automation-workflow` ranks for 8 clusters including several mismatches: "Steps To Implement Marketing Automation" (3,351 vol, pos 26) and "Examples Of Successful Marketing Automation Campaigns" (1,685 vol, pos 33). These are distinct intents that need their own pages. The workflow post itself is performing fine (pos 7.2 for its core topic) — don't change it, just add new pages for the mismatched clusters.

**Competitor comparison content is underrepresented.** "HubSpot vs Mailchimp" (5,034 vol) and "Salesforce Marketing Automation Alternatives" (4,968 vol) are both P1 topics with no existing pages. These are among the highest-ROI content types for AC because users searching these are actively evaluating tools and AC is the natural alternative to recommend. These should be the first two pieces created.

**Lead nurturing is one optimization, not six tasks.** `/blog/lead-nurturing` ranks for 6 clusters. The main cluster (Lead Nurturing Automation Process, pos 18.5) is the priority — optimizing that page should lift rankings across all 6 related clusters. Don't treat these as separate work items.

**AIO is the baseline, not a differentiator.** 85% AIO saturation means AI-generated answers are essentially universal in this topic space. The question isn't whether AIO is present — it's whether AC is cited in those answers. Upload the AIO Monitoring cluster list to thruuu to check brand citation status before committing to content production on those clusters.

**Video signals are strong throughout.** Video Feature % is above 50% on 12 of the 20 selected clusters, and at 100% on 8 of them. Any plan that defaults to article-only production is leaving format opportunities on the table. AC already has a strong /learn video infrastructure — repurposing or extending that for blog content is a natural fit.
