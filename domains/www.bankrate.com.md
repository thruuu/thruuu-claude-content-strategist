# www.bankrate.com

## Business
- **What they do**: Personal finance publisher and comparison platform providing expert-driven editorial content and comparison tools to help US consumers find the best financial products and rates.
- **Core products/features**: Credit card comparison tools, mortgage and refinance rate tables, savings and CD rate comparisons, loan calculators (mortgage, auto, personal), insurance comparison guides, 200+ calculators, editorial content on personal finance topics.
- **Target audience**: US consumers making financial decisions — choosing a credit card, refinancing a mortgage, opening a savings account, comparing insurance options.
- **Content goals**: Lead generation through affiliate referrals to financial products, brand awareness as a trusted personal finance authority, AI visibility as the cited source in financial advice queries.
- **Key competitors**: NerdWallet, Forbes Advisor, The Points Guy, Credit Karma, Investopedia.

## Site structure (from site: search)
- **Main content hubs**: /banking/, /mortgages/, /credit-cards/, /loans/, /investing/, /retirement/, /home-equity/, /insurance/, /taxes/, /calculators/, /news/, /data-center/, /real-estate/, /personal-finance/
- **Key pages observed**: Rate tables (mortgage rates, CD rates, savings APY, loan rates), 200+ calculator pages nested by topic hub, "best of" comparison articles, long-form guides and how-tos, state-specific pages (taxes, insurance), original research reports and surveys, bank/lender reviews.
- **Content patterns**: Highly topic-siloed structure. Each major product category has its own hub with articles, rates, calculators, and news nested within it. No forum or community content — purely editorial and tool-based. Rate tables double as transactional landing pages.
